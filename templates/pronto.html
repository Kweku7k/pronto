1. Payment

  • Hostel fees are to be paid in full or in installments (a minimum deposit of 75%), as agreed upon with management before occupancy can be deemed to be in effect.
  • Payment of hostel fees in full is due before the resumption of the second semester or within a month after occupying the room, whichever one comes first.
  • Late payment of hostel fees may result in additional charges, penalties, or restrictions to access to hostel services or amenities, as determined by management.
  • Failure to pay hostel fees or to comply with the terms of payment may result in termination of the occupancy agreement,eviction from the hostel and or disruption to your occupancy status.

2. Room allocation and transfer

  • Any room allocated by management is strictly non-transferable and may not be sublet or shared with other occupants without the written consent of management.
  • Occupants may request a change of room by filling out a room change request form and receiving approval from management.
  • Requests for room changes may be granted or denied at the discretion of management, based on availability, occupancy needs, and other factors.

3. Duration of occupancy

  • The duration of occupancy is for the length of the academic year as determined by the official university academic calendar, unless otherwise agreed upon in writing with management.
  • Occupants must vacate their rooms within a reasonable time (3 days) after their occupancy term has expired.
  • Occupants who wish to stay in the hostel after their occupancy term has expired must communicate their intentions to do so to management in writing at least 2 weeks prior to the end of their occupancy term and receive the appropriate written response.
  • Occupants may be allowed to store their personal belongings in their rooms during the long vacation or other breaks, subject to the availability of storage space, payment of a non-refundable reservation fee as communicated by management and the approval of management.
  • Occupants are responsible for the security and care of their personal belongings, and for arranging their own insurance or other protection as needed.

4. Utilities and Services

  • Each room will be provided with a complimentary amount of prepaid credit (GHS 300) at the commencement of the academic year.
  • Occupants are responsible for monitoring their meter usage and topping up their prepaid electricity credit as needed.
  • The hostel will not be held responsible for power outages that result from a failure of the occupant to top up their meter in a timely and sufficient manner.

5. Prohibited Items and Activities

  • Occupants are not allowed to keep firearms, explosives, ammunition, or other lethal weapons during their stay. The hostel has the right to confiscate any such items and to evict the occupants if they violate this policy.
  • Occupants are not allowed to host parties, celebrations, or festivals without the prior written consent of the management. The hostel may impose additional charges or fees for hosting events, and may require the occupants to comply with certain rules and regulations.
  • Occupants are not allowed to engage in any commercial activity without the express written consent of management. This includes, but is not limited to, selling goods or services, conducting business meetings, or operating a business from the hostel.

6. Liabilities and Responsibilities

  • The hostel will not be held liable for any damages, repairs, and or replacements that occur or are required after the occupants take charge of their allocated rooms, except in extraordinary circumstances as ascertained by the relevant artisans (e.g., plumber, electrician, etc.).
  • Occupants are responsible for maintaining their rooms in a clean and orderly condition, and for reporting any maintenance issues to the hostel in a timely manner.
  • The hostel will not be held liable for the replacement of misplaced or damaged room keys. Occupants are required to drop off their room keys and log the time of drop off and collection of said keys at the front desk/porter's lodge during breaks and vacation periods.
  • Occupants are responsible for ensuring that their room is clean, orderly, and well-maintained, and for complying with all hostel rules and regulations related to the use and care of their room.

7. Visitors and Guests

  • All non-student visitors (male and female) must present a valid nationally recognized ID at the Porter's lodge before entry. Student visitors must present only official Central University student ID cards.
  • Occupants are not allowed to host visitors (sleepovers) without the prior written consent of the management.
  • Visiting hours are from 7:00am to 8:30pm, Monday to Sunday.
  • The hostel reserves the right to refuse entry to any visitor who fails to comply with these rules or who poses a security risk.

8. Refunds and Cancellations

  • The hostel offers a full refund on the first payment or deposit if the refund is requested in writing within one week of making the payment. A 15% administrative fee will be applied to all refunds.
  • Cancellations must be made in writing and will be subject to the same administrative fee and time limits.
  • No refunds will be granted after the occupancy period has begun, except in extraordinary circumstances as determined by the hostel management.

9. Miscellaneous

  • The hostel reserves the right to update these terms and conditions at any time. It is the responsibility of the occupants to stay informed of any changes.
  • The hostel management reserves the right to evict any occupants who violate these terms and conditions or who engage in any disruptive or illegal activities.
  • Any disputes or complaints must be reported to the hostel management in writing and will be resolved in accordance with the laws of the Republic of Ghana.
